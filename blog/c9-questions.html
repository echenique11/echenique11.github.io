Asking good questions

4-05-2015

Since we started at DBC we have stumble with some difficulties accomplishing some of the challenges and wee have go on line trying to found some answers that help us with our solutions. In other to get the answers that we need or at least that we think can point us in the right direction, we need to make questions that make sense to others and not just to our self, it should be specific and self explained.
Even we should make the right questions when we get stock and want some help, we shouldn't go straight to look answers on line, but rather try to look into the documentation of the language that we are developing, for example instead of asking how to loop over an array in stack overflow directly, you should look it up in the documentation first and then if you still stuck then go look for it on line.
Once you decide to look for it on line I try to avoid how to as much as possible, for example how to loop an array in ruby, should be loop an array in ruby, because to how to questions brings too many results on tutorials, that doesn't have to do with ruby ( in this case ) precisely.
Also if you haven't find your answer in the first result page, probably you should consider reading your question and try to reformulate so you get the answer that you want.
